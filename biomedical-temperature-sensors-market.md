<p><p>Biomedical temperature sensors play a crucial role in monitoring body temperature accurately. There are several types of temperature sensors used in the market, including thermocouples, thermistors, fiber optic sensors, infrared sensors, and liquid crystal temperature sensors. Thermocouples use two different metals to measure temperature changes, while thermistors use semiconductor materials. Fiber optic sensors rely on the transmission of light to measure temperature, infrared sensors detect heat emitted by objects, and liquid crystal temperature sensors change color based on temperature fluctuations for accurate readings in biomedical applications.</p></p>
