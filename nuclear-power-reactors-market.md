<p><ul><li>Pressurized Water Reactor (PWR)</li><li>Boiling Water Reactor (BWR)</li><li>Pressurized Heavy Water Reactor (PHWR)</li><li>Fast Breeder Reactor (FBR)</li><li>Light Water Graphite Reactor (LWR)</li><li>Other</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Nuclear power reactors are classified into different types based on their design and the coolant used. The Pressurized Water Reactor (PWR) and Boiling Water Reactor (BWR) are the two most common types, where water is used as a coolant and moderator. Pressurized Heavy Water Reactors (PHWR) use heavy water as a coolant. Fast Breeder Reactors (FBR) utilize fast neutrons for sustained nuclear reaction. Light Water Graphite Reactors (LWR) use graphite as a moderator along with water coolant. There are also other reactors like Gas Cooled Reactors and Sodium Cooled Reactors in the market.</p></p>
