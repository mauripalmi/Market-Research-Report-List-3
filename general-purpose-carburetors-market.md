<p><ul><li>Float-Feed Carburetor</li><li>Diaphragm Carburetor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>General purpose carburetors are commonly categorized into two types: float-feed carburetors and diaphragm carburetors. Float-feed carburetors use a float system to control the fuel levels in the carburetor bowl, ensuring a consistent fuel flow to the engine. On the other hand, diaphragm carburetors use a flexible diaphragm to regulate fuel intake, making them more suitable for smaller engines. Both types play a vital role in controlling the air-fuel mixture in internal combustion engines for optimal performance.</p></p>
