<p><p>The marine internal combustion engine market can be classified into three types based on their operating speeds: Low-speed engines, medium-speed engines, and high-speed engines. Low-speed engines typically run at speeds below 300 RPM and are commonly used in large ships and tankers. Medium-speed engines operate between 300 to 1000 RPM and are often found in ferries and fishing vessels. High-speed engines, on the other hand, run above 1000 RPM and are commonly used in yachts and small boats.</p></p>
