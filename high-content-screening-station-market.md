<p><p>High Content Screening Stations are advanced automated systems used in drug discovery and research. These stations come in different types, including Brightfield and Fluorescence markets. Brightfield stations use white light to capture high-resolution images, making them ideal for studying cellular morphology. Fluorescence stations, on the other hand, use fluorescent dyes to visualize specific cellular components or processes with high sensitivity. Both types of stations have their own advantages and are widely used in pharmaceutical companies and research laboratories for screening and analyzing large numbers of cellular samples.</p></p>
