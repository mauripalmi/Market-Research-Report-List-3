<p><p>Spinal cages are devices used in spinal fusion surgeries to maintain disc height and relieve pressure on nerves. Two common types of materials used in spinal cages are polyetheretherketone (PEEK) and titanium. PEEK cages are known for their radiolucency and ability to closely match the mechanical properties of bone, while titanium cages provide excellent strength and biocompatibility. Surgeons may choose between these two materials based on the specific needs of the patient and the surgical procedure.</p></p>
