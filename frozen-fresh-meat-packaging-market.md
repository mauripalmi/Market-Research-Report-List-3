<p><p>Two common types of packaging for frozen fresh meat are Vacuum Packaging and Modified Atmosphere Packaging. Vacuum packaging involves removing air from the package to prevent oxidation and spoilage. This method extends the shelf life of the meat by reducing exposure to oxygen. Modified Atmosphere Packaging involves replacing the air in the package with a mixture of gases to create an optimal environment for preserving the meat. This type of packaging helps maintain the quality and freshness of the meat for a longer period of time.</p></p>
