<p><p>The hydronic underfloor heating system market is dominated by two main types: dry water floor heating and wet water floor heating. Dry water floor heating involves embedding water pipes within a dry screed or insulation layer, providing efficient heat transfer. Wet water floor heating, on the other hand, involves placing water pipes within a wet screed or concrete layer for higher thermal mass. Both types offer effective and energy-efficient heating solutions for homes and commercial buildings, catering to different preferences and requirements.</p></p>
