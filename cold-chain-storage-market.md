<p><p>Cold chain storage refers to the specialized storage and transportation of temperature-sensitive products to maintain their quality and freshness. There are two main types of cold chain storage: chilled and frozen markets. Chilled storage maintains temperatures above freezing, typically between 0 to 8 degrees Celsius, to preserve perishable items like fruits, vegetables, and dairy products. Frozen storage, on the other hand, maintains temperatures below freezing, typically between -18 to -30 degrees Celsius, to preserve items like meat, seafood, and ice cream. Each market type serves a different set of products with specific temperature requirements.</p></p>
