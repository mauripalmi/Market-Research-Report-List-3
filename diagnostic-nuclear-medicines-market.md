<p><p>Diagnostic nuclear medicine refers to the use of radioactive tracers to diagnose and monitor various medical conditions. The three main types of diagnostic nuclear medicines are Tc-99m, F-18, and other radiotracers. Tc-99m is the most widely used radiotracer due to its ideal characteristics for imaging various organs and systems in the body. F-18 is commonly used for PET scans, offering high-resolution images for detecting cancer and other diseases. Other radiotracers are used for specific imaging purposes based on their properties and targets within the body.</p></p>
